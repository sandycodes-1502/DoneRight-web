The visual identity of DoneRight relies on a "Warm Minimalist" theme, transitioning away from heavy dark modes toward a highly breathable, light-dominant canvas with stark, high-contrast typography.

Color Palette

Primary Base (Background): Warm Off-White (#F7F5F2 approx.). Used for the main body background to reduce eye strain and provide a soft, premium feel.

Surface Level (Cards & Containers): Pure White (#FFFFFF). Used for the central task dashboard, feature cards, and input fields to create separation from the warm base without relying on heavy drop shadows.

Inverted Base (High Emphasis): Soft Black / Deep Charcoal (#1A1A1A). Used for the primary buttons ("Get started"), the bottom CTA section background, and footer, creating strong structural anchors.

Primary Text: Soft Black (#1A1A1A). Used for all main headings and primary task titles for maximum legibility.

Secondary Text: Muted Grey (#6B6B6B). Used for subtitles, descriptions, and placeholder text to establish visual hierarchy without clutter.

Accent (Success & Progress): Muted Forest Green (#3A7D44 approx.). Used sparingly for the status pill dot, completed task checkmarks, and the progress bar to provide positive reinforcement without breaking the minimalist aesthetic.

Typography & Hierarchy

Typeface: A clean, modern sans-serif (e.g., Inter, Roboto, or SF Pro).

Hero Headings: Extra Large, Semi-Bold/Bold. Tightly tracked for impact (e.g., "Get things done, the right way").

Section Headings: Large, Medium/Semi-Bold. Used for feature titles and steps (e.g., "Quick Capture").

Body Copy: Regular weight, high line-height for readability. Used for descriptions.

Micro-copy: Small, Medium weight. Used for category tags (e.g., "Work", "Health") displayed in light grey pill containers.

UI Elements & Styling

Borders & Dividers: Very subtle, light grey (#E5E5E5) lines used to separate task items and define the edges of the white surface cards.

Corner Radius: Medium-soft rounding (approx. 8px - 12px) on buttons, cards, and status pills, avoiding aggressive sharp edges to maintain an approachable feel.

Shadows: Extremely minimal. The design relies more on color contrast (Pure White on Warm Off-White) than drop shadows to establish depth.
